Console table, two tone nested legs, walnut top over an ebonized oak base. I really like how this one turned out.

## Materials

- Top: walnut, waterfall from a few edge glued boards
- Legs & apron: red oak, ebonized black
- Finish: Rubio Monocoat Pure on the walnut, Rubio Monocoat Black precolor easy on the oak base
- Joinery: Domino floating tenons

## Build notes

<Photo src={glueUp} alt="Console table assembled and sanded, before any finish" caption="Assembled and sanded, before finish — two open shelves, two part legs, 1/8 inch roundover on waterfall edge" />

This was my first waterfall edge, and it intimidated me a bit. I ended up using dominos to align the walnut boards into a panel, and using a track saw to make the 45 degree cuts at the waterfall edge. I also applied a 60 degree bevel to the front bottom edge, and a 1/8" roundover on the top edge.

I free hand routed out a pocket in the waterfall top short side to accept the oak leg piece (No good pics). I think were I to do it again, I would create a template for this to get a more precise fit. There are bigger gaps than I would like. 

I think 2 dividers in the top section with a live edge fronted set of drawers would have been a nice addition to this piece

<Photo src={ebonizing} alt="Close-up of the ebonized oak leg against the still-raw walnut top" caption="Ebonizing the oak base — black precolor easy and a coat of Rubio applied, before the top got attached and its own finish" />

I think Foureyes furniture or Sean Boyd has a couple pieces that inspired this for sure. The black/walnut is striking: black oak base, walnut left natural so the grain does the work. I thought about trying ebonizing using steel wool and ammonia but opted for black Rubio precolor easy and black Rubio on the red oak. It worked pretty well for this since the open grain takes the stain evenly and still reads as texture under the black.

<Photo src={topGrain} alt="Close-up of the walnut tabletop grain after finishing" caption="Walnut top after the oil finish — grain came out great" />

Applied the precolor easy with a red scotch bright pad, then applied the rubio with a bondo scraper followed by a white scotch bright pad. The walnut turned out great and the grain on the oak really adds a nice texture. Overall pretty happy with it, and my finishing skills are getting a little better. Less blotchy every time.
