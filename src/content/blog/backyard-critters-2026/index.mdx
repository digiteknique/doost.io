No woodworking or scuba this time, just a few backyard finds worth pointing a camera at.

## Praying mantis

This one showed up on the screen last September, right around dusk, with the grill in the background as a size reference. It sat there long enough for a full photo session — front legs raised the whole time, either tracking something or just posing.

## Walking stick

Found this one hanging out on a window screen back in June. Northern walking sticks (*Diapheromera femorata*) are common around here but I don't run into them up close very often — they're built to disappear into twigs and grass, and the screen mesh made for a nice contrasting background to actually see the thing.

<Photo src={stickInsect1} alt="Walking stick insect clinging to a window screen, legs splayed out in an X shape" caption="First one to show up — legs splayed into an X against the mesh" />

<Photo src={stickInsect2} alt="Walking stick insect on a window screen, viewed from the side" caption="Legs longer than the body, doing a very convincing impression of a stray blade of grass" />

<Photo src={stickInsect3} alt="Walking stick insect fully extended vertically on a window screen" caption="Fully stretched out — the camouflage really only fails against a grid background like this" />

## Orb weaver

This one showed up in a porch corner and has been rebuilding its web there for a couple weeks now. Bold yellow-and-black banding, and a zigzag stitching — a stabilimentum — woven down the center of the web.

<Photo src={orbWeaver1} alt="Orb weaver spider on its web in a porch corner, with a smaller spider visible nearby" caption="Working the web in the corner — looks like a smaller spider has staked out the other side" />

<Photo src={orbWeaver2} alt="Orb weaver spider centered on its web against brick and siding" caption="The zigzag stabilimentum running through the center of the web — still not fully settled on what it's for, but candidates are prey lures, web stabilization, or making the web visible enough that birds don't fly through it" />

<Photo src={orbWeaver3} alt="Close-up of orb weaver spider showing black and yellow banded legs and abdomen" caption="Close enough to see the banding on the legs" />

<Photo src={orbWeaver4} alt="Orb weaver spider on web spanning a porch corner" caption="Same spider, a few days later, web rebuilt after the last one presumably caught something or got wrecked by weather" />

Orb weavers rebuild a good chunk of their web most nights, so if it's still here next week I'll probably have more photos.
