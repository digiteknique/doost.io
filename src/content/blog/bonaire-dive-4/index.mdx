Only dive of the trip not on Bellafonte's house reef. Salt Pier is a working pier - Bonaire's salt company still loads ships from it - so you can only dive it when there's no ship docked, which makes it feel a little more occasion than a regular shore dive.

## Notes

The pilings are the whole dive - dozens of them, encrusted floor to surface with sponges and coral, standing in these long parallel rows. Swimming through them with sunlight cutting down between the pilings was the best moment of the whole trip.

## Marine life

Less about the fish here and more about the structure itself - the pilings are their own little reef, thick with sponges and coral growth after decades underwater.
